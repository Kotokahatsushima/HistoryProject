Must-Visit Places to Immerse Yourself in the Heart of Tradition and Natural Beauty

Researched By: Iva Latogan

Edited by: Kadmiel Pagaduan

Must Visit Places in Benguet

Mount Costa

  • Mount Costa, nestled in Benguet, Philippines, is a captivating retreat that intertwines nature and artistry. This haven boasts themed gardens that weave a tapestry of enchanting landscapes. Visitors traverse fairytale-inspired realms adorned with vibrant flowers and whimsical sculptures, or find solace in serene spaces embraced by lush greenery. Beyond its visual allure, Mount Costa fosters a deeper connection to nature, emphasizing the delicate interplay between human creativity and the environment. It stands as a testament to the transformative power of landscapes, offering a respite from the ordinary and an immersive journey into the harmonious convergence of art and nature. For those seeking a refreshing escape, Mount Costa in Benguet promises a brief but enchanting sojourn where every corner reveals the magic of this unique fusion.

    • Mount Pulag

    • Standing proudly as the third-highest peak in the Philippines, Mount Pulag is a paradise for hikers and nature enthusiasts. The journey to its summit unveils diverse ecosystems, from mossy forests to grasslands. The real magic happens at dawn when the sea of clouds blankets the landscape, offering a celestial experience. Mount Pulag is not merely a mountain; it's a pilgrimage for those seeking solace in nature's grandeur.

    Strawberry Farm

    • La Trinidad's strawberry fields paint the landscape in hues of red and green, creating a picturesque scene that captivates the senses. The sweet aroma of fresh strawberries fills the air as visitors engage in the delightful activity of strawberry picking. The Strawberry Festival in March transforms the area into a lively celebration of culture, agriculture, and community, making it a must-visit for those seeking a taste of local life.

    Tam-awan Village

    • Tam-awan Village, nestled in the mountains of Baguio City, is a cultural sanctuary preserving the Cordilleran heritage. The village's huts, constructed with indigenous materials, transport visitors to a bygone era. Art exhibits and cultural shows showcase the creative spirit of the Cordillera region. It's not just a village; it's a living canvas that tells the story of a vibrant and resilient community.

    BenCab Museum

    • Perched on a hill in Tuba, the BenCab Museum is a masterpiece of art and architecture. Founded by national artist Benedicto Cabrera, the museum houses a diverse collection that transcends time and tradition. Each painting, sculpture, and artifact tells a story, inviting visitors into the rich tapestry of Philippine culture. The museum's design harmonizes with nature, offering an immersive experience that goes beyond visual aesthetics.

    Asin Hotsprings

    • Tucked away in the heart of Tuba, Asin Hot Springs provides a therapeutic escape surrounded by nature's embrace. The natural sulfuric hot spring pools offer relaxation and rejuvenation. The cool mountain breeze complements the warm waters, creating a serene atmosphere. It's not just a hot spring; it's a sanctuary where visitors can unwind and reconnect with the healing forces of nature.

    Mines View Park

    • Mines View Park, a cherished destination in Baguio, offers more than just a scenic overlook. The panoramic views of the mining town and surrounding mountains set the stage for a cultural experience. Visitors can shop for local handicrafts, providing a glimpse into the region's artistic traditions. Donning traditional Cordilleran attire adds a touch of authenticity to the immersive Mines View Park experience.
