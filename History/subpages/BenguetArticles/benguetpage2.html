Traditions of Benguet

Ritual and Ceremonies

  • Loom Weaving The Sayaw ritual is a traditional dance performed during significant occasions such as weddings, feasts, and celebrations. It often involves intricate footwork and hand movements that convey the community's unity and harmony.
  • Ebeg: Harvest Ritual The Ebeg is a thanksgiving ritual performed during the harvest season. It includes chanting, dancing, and the offering of agricultural products to deities as a way of expressing gratitude for a bountiful harvest.

Cordillera Weaving

  • Loom Weaving Benguet is known for its traditional weaving, particularly the intricate designs created by the indigenous women using backstrap looms. The fabrics produced are often vibrant and represent the weaver's skills and cultural identity.
  • Tapis and WannoTraditional clothing like the tapis (wrap-around skirt) and wanno (g-string) are still worn by women during cultural events. These garments are often adorned with symbolic patterns that convey social status and roles within the community.

Language and Oral Tradition

  • Kankana-ey Language The Kankana-ey language is widely spoken in Benguet. It is an essential aspect of cultural identity, preserving the unique worldview and wisdom of the community. Elders often pass down stories, myths, and proverbs through oral tradition.

Festivals

  • Panagbenga FestivalThis is the annual flower festival of Benguet, celebrated in Baguio City. It showcases vibrant floats adorned with flowers and traditional dances. The festival is a celebration of the region's rich cultural heritage and its blooming flower industry.

Religious Beliefs:

  • Anito Worship: Traditional religious practices often involve the worship of anitos, ancestral spirits believed to inhabit natural elements like rocks, trees, and rivers. Offerings and rituals are conducted to seek protection, guidance, and blessings from these spirits.

Agricultural Practices:

  • Terrace Farming The rugged landscape of Benguet is transformed into an agricultural marvel through the use of ancient terrace farming techniques. These rice terraces are not only practical for cultivating crops but also hold cultural significance, symbolizing the community's cooperation and industry.

Social Practices:

  • Bodong: This is a traditional peace pact or agreement that has been practiced for generations. It serves as a means of conflict resolution and promotes harmony among different communities.

Environmental Stewardship:

  • Ling-ay: The Ling-ay concept embodies the traditional Cordilleran principle of environmental stewardship. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of humans and nature, promoting sustainable practices and respect for the environment.
