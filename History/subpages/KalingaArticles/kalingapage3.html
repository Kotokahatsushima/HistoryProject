Discovering Kalinga: From Ancient Terraces to Historical Marvels

By: Darly Rollog Ebreo

Here are some places that were notable attractions in Kalinga:

Tinglayan Rice Terraces (also known as the Tinglayan Rice Terraces and Banaue Rice Terraces)

Similar to the more famous Banaue Rice Terraces, these terraces are a marvel of ancient engineering and showcase the traditional farming practices of the indigenous people.

Buscalan Village

Home to the famous Whang-Od, a traditional Kalinga tattoo artist, Buscalan Village has become a pilgrimage site for those seeking traditional hand-tapped tattoos.

Tabuk City

The capital of Kalinga, Tabuk City offers a mix of modern amenities and cultural experiences. The city is known for its historical sites, including the Kalinga Capitol Grounds and the Provincial Museum.

Chico River

For adventure seekers, the Chico River is a popular destination for whitewater rafting. The scenic landscapes along the river add to the thrill of the experience.

Mount Balbalasang National Park

This national park is known for its diverse flora and fauna. It's a great destination for nature lovers, offering hiking trails and opportunities for bird watching.

Sumaguing Cave

Although primarily known as part of the Sagada Cave Connection in Mountain Province, Sumaguing Cave is often visited by tourists exploring the northern Luzon region, including Kalinga.

Kalinga, like many other provinces, might produce a variety of crops such as rice, corn, vegetables, and fruits.

Here are some facts about Kalinga:

Geography

Kalinga is historically associated with the coastal regions of present-day Odisha and parts of northern Andhra Pradesh, India. It has a diverse geography, including coastal areas, hills, and plains.

Ancient History

Kalinga has a significant place in ancient Indian history. It was mentioned in various ancient texts, including the Mahabharata and the Puranas.

Kalinga War

One of the most notable events associated with Kalinga is the Kalinga War, fought in the 3rd century BCE. The Mauryan Emperor Ashoka waged this war, and its consequences had a profound impact on him. The extreme bloodshed and suffering led Ashoka to renounce violence and embrace Buddhism.

Buddhism

The aftermath of the Kalinga War played a crucial role in the spread of Buddhism. Cultural Heritage

Kalinga has a rich cultural heritage, including classical dance forms like Odissi, which originated in the region. The architecture of the temples in Bhubaneswar and Konark is another testament to the cultural and artistic legacy of Kalinga.

Temples

The state of Odisha, which includes the ancient Kalinga region, is known for its magnificent temples. The Lingaraja Temple in Bhubaneswar, the Jagannath Temple in Puri, and the Sun Temple in Konark are among the most famous temples in India.
