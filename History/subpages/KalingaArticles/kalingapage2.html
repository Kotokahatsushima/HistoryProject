Exploring the Rich Heritage: A Glimpse into the History, Culture, and Language of Kalinga

By: Darly Rollog Ebreo

History of Kalinga

Kalinga was first organized as a political realm under the American political rule when Mountain Province was created by the Philippine Commission Act 1876 on August 18, 1912. It was one of the five sub-provinces then of Mountain Province when it was divided into several, sub-provincial units along ethnic lines.

Kalinga is a province in the northern part of the Philippines, known for its rich cultural heritage, distinct traditions, and vibrant indigenous communities. Here are some aspects of Kalinga culture, language, and its administrative status:

Culture:

Tattooing Tradition: Kalinga is famous for its traditional tattooing, particularly among the Kalinga people. The practice involves intricate designs that signify bravery, beauty, and social status. Women with tattoos are highly regarded in Kalinga society.

Rice Terraces:

Like many other regions in the Philippines, Kalinga is characterized by stunning rice terraces. These terraces are not only functional for agriculture but also serve as a testament to the ingenuity of the indigenous people in managing their mountainous environment.

Rituals and Festivals:

Kalinga people celebrate various rituals and festivals that reflect their cultural beliefs and practices. These events often involve traditional dances, music, and ceremonies that highlight their connection to nature, ancestors, and spirituality.

Language:

The primary language spoken in Kalinga is Kalinga, which belongs to the Malayo-Polynesian language family. However, specific dialects may vary among different communities within the province. English and Filipino (the national language) are also commonly spoken and used in education and official communication.
