Embracing Diversity in Unity: The Resilient People of Abra and Their Cultural Mosaic

By: Jayson Martin

The People

Abra's strength lies in its resilient and inclusive people. Despite the diversity of ethnic groups, the Tingguians, who form a significant indigenous community, coexist harmoniously with others. Each community has its customs and languages, yet they unite in a beautiful symphony of traditions, showcasing the province's remarkable unity in diversity. The Ilocanos, Tingguians, and Igorots are the backbone of Abra's society, preserving its rich cultural heritage.

  • Ilocanos - Abra is home to the Ilocanos, who make up the majority population of the lowland communities. The province has benefited from its warm and welcoming nature, which has added to its cultural diversity. The vibrant traditions and strong sense of community of the Ilocanos have enriched the province's tapestry of cultures.
  • Tingguians - The province of Abra is home to a variety of indigenous peoples, including the Kankanaey, Isnag, and Balatok tribes, who bring color and diversity to the region. These tribes have been the guardians of the land since ancient times, and their customs and traditions are deeply intertwined with the province's culture. For centuries, they have coexisted with nature, utilizing effective agricultural practices and preserving their way of life.
  • Igorots - The Igorots have unique cultural practices, languages, and a deep connection to their ancestral lands. They contribute to the province's cultural diversity.
