Researched By: Joemil Mangili

Etag Festival

  • The Etag Festival, celebrated by the Bontoc people in Mountain Province, Philippines, is a vibrant cultural event deeply rooted in the community's agricultural traditions. This annual festival revolves around the ritualistic curing and communal sharing of etag, a traditional salted and cured meat. The festival serves as a joyful expression of gratitude for a successful harvest, fostering unity among the community. During the Etag Festival, locals gather to showcase their culinary skills in preparing etag, which is often made from smoked and salted pork or beef. The curing process, a traditional preservation method, not only ensures a stable food supply during lean months but also highlights the sustainable agricultural practices of the Bontoc people. Through dances, ceremonies, and shared meals, the Etag Festival stands as a testament to the deep cultural connection between the Bontoc community and the bountiful landscapes that sustain them.

Solkong Ritual

  • The Solkong Ritual, deeply ingrained in Kankanaey culture in the verdant landscapes of Mountain Province, Philippines, is a sacred ceremony performed to beckon blessings from deities for a prosperous harvest. This cultural spectacle involves a harmonious interplay of offerings, traditional dances, and heartfelt prayers, all orchestrated to express gratitude for past agricultural yields and to seek divine guidance for the forthcoming cycles. The Kankanaey people, with reverence and devotion, partake in the Solkong Ritual, fostering a profound connection between their spiritual beliefs and the agricultural practices that sustain their communities. As the rhythmic dances echo through the mountains, and offerings are made to the gods of the harvest, the Solkong Ritual encapsulates the essence of Kankanaey identity, celebrating the intricate relationship between the people and the land that nourishes them.

Begnas Ritual

  • The Begnas Ritual, an integral facet of Cordilleran culture in the breathtaking landscapes of Mountain Province, Philippines, is a communal celebration that transcends time. Practiced by the Kankanaey and other indigenous groups, Begnas is a profound expression of gratitude to the spirits for bountiful harvests and a plea for guidance in future agricultural endeavors. Amidst vibrant ceremonies, dances, and traditional offerings, the ritual unfolds as a harmonious dialogue between the community and the divine forces that govern the fertility of their lands. Begnas encapsulates the deep respect these communities hold for nature, reflecting an understanding of the symbiotic relationship between mankind and the environment. This cultural tapestry, woven through the ages, illustrates the enduring significance of Begnas, symbolizing a timeless connection between the people of Mountain Province and the cycles of nature that sustain their way of life.
